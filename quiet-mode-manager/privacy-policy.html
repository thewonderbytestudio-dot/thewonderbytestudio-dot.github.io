Privacy Policy for Quiet Mode Manager

Effective date: June 1, 2026

Quiet Mode Manager is developed by The WonderByte Studio. This Privacy Policy explains how the app handles information when you use Quiet Mode Manager.

Information We Collect

Quiet Mode Manager is designed to work locally on your Android device. The current version of the app does not require an account and does not upload your sound profiles, volume settings, schedules, or custom modes to a server.

The app may store the following information locally on your device:

  • Your saved custom sound mode name
  • Your saved ring, media, and alarm volume settings
  • Your selected ringer behavior, such as Sound, Vibrate, or Silent
  • Your saved schedule preview settings if Pro features are enabled in a future version

This information is stored locally on your device and is used only to make the app function.

Permissions and Device Settings

Quiet Mode Manager may interact with your device sound settings so it can apply sound profiles such as Work Mode, Sleep Mode, Focus Mode, and custom modes.

The app may adjust:

  • Ring volume
  • Media volume
  • Alarm volume
  • Ringer behavior, such as Sound, Vibrate, or Silent, where supported by the device

Quiet Mode Manager does not use these sound settings to identify you.

Data Sharing

Quiet Mode Manager does not sell your personal information. The current version of the app does not share your saved sound profiles, custom modes, or schedule settings with third parties.

Internet and Accounts

The current MVP version of Quiet Mode Manager does not require a user account and does not require cloud storage for saved modes.

In-App Purchases

Quiet Mode Manager may offer a one-time Pro unlock in the future. If in-app purchases are added, purchase processing will be handled by Google Play. The app developer does not directly receive your full payment card information.

Data Retention and Deletion

Locally saved app settings remain on your device unless you delete them in the app, clear the app data, or uninstall the app.

You can delete saved custom modes inside the app. You can also remove locally stored data by uninstalling Quiet Mode Manager or clearing the app’s storage through Android settings.

Children’s Privacy

Quiet Mode Manager is not directed toward children under 13. The app is intended as a general utility for managing sound profiles on Android devices.

Security

Quiet Mode Manager stores its current app settings locally on your device. No method of storage or transmission is completely secure, but the current MVP does not upload your saved sound profiles to an external server.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.
